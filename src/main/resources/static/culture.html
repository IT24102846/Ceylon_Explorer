🙏 Respect & Understand

Cultural Etiquette Guide

Sri Lanka is a deeply spiritual country. Understanding local customs ensures respectful and meaningful interactions.

🛕
Temples & Religious Sites
Buddhist, Hindu & Sacred Spaces
DO Remove shoes before entering any temple or shrine
DO Dress modestly — shoulders and knees covered (sarongs often provided)
DO Walk clockwise around stupas and sacred trees
DON'T Turn your back to a Buddha statue for photos
DON'T Pose with Buddha statues — it's considered disrespectful
DON'T Enter temples during prayer times uninvited
NOTE Menstruating women may be restricted from some Hindu temples
🤝
Greetings & Social Customs
Connecting with Locals
DO Greet with "Āyubōvan" (Sinhala) or "Vanakkam" (Tamil) with hands pressed together
DO Use your right hand for handshakes and passing items
DO Respect elders — address them with titles
DON'T Shake hands with monks — press palms together instead
DON'T Touch anyone on the head — it's considered sacred
NOTE Public displays of affection are frowned upon
🍛
Dining Etiquette
Eating the Lankan Way
DO Eat with your right hand — a sign of respect and tradition
DO Finish what's on your plate — leaving food can seem wasteful
DO Wait for the eldest or host to start eating first
DON'T Use your left hand to eat or pass food — considered unclean
DON'T Refuse an invitation to share food — very rude
NOTE Many Sri Lankans are vegetarian on Poya (full moon) days
👗
Dress Code
Dressing Respectfully
DO Dress conservatively in villages and religious areas
DO Swimwear is fine at beaches — change before leaving
DO Cover tattoos of Buddha or Hindu deities in public
DON'T Wear revealing clothing in cities or sacred places
DON'T Enter government buildings in shorts
NOTE Light, breathable fabrics recommended in humid climate
📷
Photography
Capturing Memories Respectfully
DO Ask permission before photographing locals, especially in villages
DO Smile and show the photo — it creates connection!
DON'T Photograph military installations or checkpoints
DON'T Take photos inside temples without permission
DON'T Use flash near sacred artifacts
NOTE Some sites charge a photography fee — check in advance
🐘
Wildlife & Nature
Responsible Tourism
DO Stay in vehicles during safari and follow guide instructions
DO Use responsible operators certified by the Wildlife Dept.
DON'T Ride elephants — many are kept in harmful conditions
DON'T Feed wild animals in parks
DON'T Remove coral, shells, or natural materials from beaches
NOTE National park entry requires valid permits booked in advance

🌤️ When to Visit

Best Season to Visit Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka's climate varies by region and time of year. Here's your complete seasonal guide.

☀️ Dry Season West
December – March
Peak season for the southwest coast, cultural triangle, and hill country. Clear skies, minimal rain, perfect beach weather. Busiest and most expensive period.
Best ForGalleColomboKandySigiriyaElla
🌺 Shoulder Season
April – May
Transitional period with warm weather and occasional showers. Prices drop slightly. April hosts New Year celebrations — a wonderful cultural experience.
Best ForCultural TriangleEast CoastFestivals
🌊 Dry Season East
May – September
The east coast comes alive — Trincomalee, Arugam Bay, Batticaloa. Perfect for surfing (Arugam Bay peak: July–Sep), diving, and whale watching in Trinco.
Best ForArugam BayTrincomaleeSurfingDiving
🍃 Monsoon Season
June – Nov (SW), Oct–Jan (NE)
Lush green landscapes, fewer tourists, lower prices. Rain is usually short afternoon bursts. Not suitable for west/south coast beaches during SW monsoon.
Best ForBudget TravelPhotographyAyurveda Retreats
